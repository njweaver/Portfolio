    Ohio's Opioid and Foster Crisis Geocoding and Heatmapping

Project Summary

The objective of this project was to answer the question of how has the opioid crisis has impacted the foster care system in the state of Ohio. Thanks to data from the Ohio Department of Health I was able to get all the opiod overdose deaths from the years 2008 to 2017. Combined with data from the Annie E. Casey Foundation Kids Count Data Center, I was able to establish a significant correlation between overdose deaths and children entering foster care.

Interestingly enough, while a state-wide correlation was found, the counties that have the highest overdose death rates aren't where the most kids entering foster care live. It is the most populous counties of Franklin, Hamilton, and Cuyahoga that are driving the increase in foster children. This could be explained by multiple reasons, including: differences in the quality of each counties Child Welfare Service, an increase in deadliness of an overdose in rural counties, or differences in family life in rural vs urban counties.
